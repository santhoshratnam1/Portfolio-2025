üìã Project Overview

Mechanical Fury is a fast-paced, single-player movement shooter emphasizing high-octane combat and vertical platforming within destructible mechanical environments. The game combines intense action mechanics similar to Doom Eternal and Ghostrunner with a protagonist equipped with a mechanical arm capable of grappling, kicking, and wielding diverse weapon systems against hordes of mechanical enemies in a dystopian sci-fi setting.

üéØ The Challenge

The primary challenge was to design a level that supports both high-speed traversal and precision combat without overwhelming the player. The environment needed to be readable at high speeds while providing enough tactical depth for complex enemy encounters. Additionally, the project required a robust technical foundation to support procedural elements and heavy VFX loads while maintaining 60 FPS.

üîß Design Process

1

Phase 1: Conceptualization & Blockout

Started with paper maps and flowcharts to define the pacing and encounter beats. Moved to greyboxing in Unreal Engine to test scale and movement metrics.

2

Phase 2: Procedural Systems

Developed the "Modular Encounter System" and procedural tools.

Key Tool: Created a blueprint-based wall and floor generator that allowed for rapid iteration of level geometry without manual asset placement.

3

Phase 3: Encounter Design

Implemented the "Three-Phase Encounter" structure. Scripted enemy spawn patterns and triggers to ensure players were constantly pushed to move.

4

Phase 4: VFX & Polish

Integrated Niagara particle systems for weapons, impacts, and environmental effects. Added lighting and post-processing to establish the dystopian atmosphere.

5

Phase 5: Optimization

Profiled performance and implemented object pooling and LODs to hit the 60 FPS target.

Memory Management: Implemented object pooling for all particle effects and temporary game objects. Reduced memory allocation by 60% during gameplay.

Frame Rate: Maintained consistent 60 FPS on target hardware (mid-range PCs) even with maximum VFX running simultaneously.
